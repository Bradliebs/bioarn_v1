Figure 1: System architecture diagram for Bio-ARN 2.0.

Purpose:
Show the full closed-loop system, emphasizing that Bio-ARN is not a feed-forward classifier but an embodied recurrent architecture.

Layout:
- Top block: Global Neuronal Workspace (GNW), shown as a small-capacity broadcast strip with 7 slots.
- Middle block: multiple Concept Cell Clusters (CCCs) arranged in parallel columns.
- Below CCCs: Associative Fabric / Sparse Distributed Memory layer connecting CCCs horizontally.
- Below memory: Predictive Engine (PE) shown as a hierarchy with top-down predictions and bottom-up errors.
- Bottom block: Embodied Sensorimotor Cortex with vision, text/language, audio, touch, and motor substreams.
- Side block: Reward and Novelty System feeding modulation arrows into CCCs, PE, and GNW.

Key arrows:
1. Sensory input -> sparse spiking encoders.
2. Prediction errors ascend from sensorimotor streams into the PE.
3. CCCs receive sparse feature patterns from the PE/sensory layer.
4. Active CCCs enter GNW.
5. GNW broadcast re-enters the Associative Fabric and PE.
6. Motor predictions descend back to the language/motor stream.
7. Action/output feeds back as new sensory input.

Caption suggestion:
"Bio-ARN 2.0 combines sparse sensory encoding, margin-gated concept recognition, Kanerva-style associative memory, predictive coding, a bounded global workspace, and reward-modulated local learning inside a closed sensorimotor loop."
